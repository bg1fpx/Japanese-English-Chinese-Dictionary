You can press Ctrl + S to save this dictionary to your computer for offline use.

This is a minimalistic trilingual dictionary. You can enter a Japanese word to look up its equivalent Chinese word(s), and vice versa.

Enter a keyword to look up all the words starting with it, for example, 大 > 大風 | 大雨 | 大雪.

Add a period (.) before a keyword to look up all the words containing it, for example, .日 > 昨日 | 今日 | 明日.

Click ::: to turn on/off a Japanese keypad on which you can enter hiragana and katakana.

Before the lookup, you must enter at least 2 kanas or 1 kanji, for example, 富 or 富士 or 富士山 > 富士山.

The following numbers are used to represent the accents of Japanese words (L=Low, H=High): 
(0) L H H H H ...
(1) H L L L L ...
(2) L H L
(3) L H H L
(4) L H H H L
(5) L H H H H L
and so on for the numbers greater than 5
The following abbreviations are used to represent the types of Japanese words: 
[a] adjective (形容詞)
[ad] adverb (副詞)
[adn] adnominal (連体詞)
[adt] totaru adverb (とたる副詞)
[an] adjectival noun (形容動詞)
[c] conjunction (接続詞)
[int] interjection (感動詞)
[n] noun (名詞)
[num] numeral (数詞)
[p] pronoun (代名詞)
[pa] particle (助詞)
[pref] prefix (接頭詞)
[suf] suffix (接尾詞)
[vg] godan verb (五段動詞)
[vi] ichidan verb (一段動詞)
[vk] kuru verb (くる動詞)
[vm] modal verb (助動詞)
[vs] suru verb (する動詞)
Click the plus sign (+) after an entry, you can see the translation provided by Google or Bing or Yandex in a new window.
